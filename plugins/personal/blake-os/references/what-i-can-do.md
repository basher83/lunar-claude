# What blake-os can do for you

These tools keep your project safe so you never lose your work — and you don't
need to know anything about how they do it. This note explains, in plain terms,
what you can ask for. Have your Claude read it and walk you through it.

## The short version

Whenever you want your work protected, just say so in normal words. Behind the
scenes your project gets saved and copied up to GitHub (an online backup), and
you're always told what happened in plain language. You can't break anything —
the tools never delete your work, never do anything risky, and stop and explain
themselves if they're ever unsure.

## What you can ask for

| When you want to… | Just say something like… |
|-------------------|--------------------------|
| Keep your work safe (save it and back it up online) | "back it up", "save my work", "I'm done for the day" |
| Get the newest version (if you use more than one computer, or Brent changed something) | "get the latest", "did anything change?" |
| Check whether everything's saved | "is it saved?", "is this backed up?" |

You don't have to use these exact words — say it however feels natural. If your
Claude isn't sure what you meant, it does the safest thing, which is usually to
save and back everything up.

## If something's confusing or goes wrong

If anything trips you up, doesn't work the way you expected, or you just wish it
worked differently — say so. Your Claude quietly passes a note along to Brent,
who looks after these tools and uses what you tell him to make them better. You
don't have to do anything special; just mention it.

## You don't need to remember any of this

That's the whole point. Say what you want in your own words, and the tools take
care of the rest.
